Clock Synchronization Is a Nightmare

The article surveys clock drift, skew, and ordering failures across builds, databases, tracing, NTP, PTP, logical clocks, vector clocks, and Spanner TrueTime. The useful model is bounded uncertainty: physical clocks can support distributed guarantees only when the system carries explicit uncertainty or shifts ordering to causality-aware logical time.

  • Clock skew breaks build systems, databases, and traces by making effects appear before causes.
  • NTP works for millisecond-class synchronization, while PTP needs hardware timestamping and network support for sub-microsecond precision.
  • Logical and vector clocks model causality without depending on one global physical timestamp.
  • TrueTime-style APIs expose an uncertainty interval, which lets systems wait or reason before committing ordering-sensitive work.
