Pluralistic: In praise of (some) compartmentalization (14 Apr 2026)

Doctorow separates useful compartmentalization from passive escape by contrasting goal-directed flow with the low-purpose distraction he calls zombie flow. It is worth keeping because it offers a durable model for directing attention under stress without drifting into AI-slop habits or using work to avoid necessary emotional labor.

  • Flow is most useful when it has a clear goal and an achievable challenge; passive scrolling removes purpose while preserving the trance.
  • Compartmentalization helps when it cools reactive emotion and makes deliberate action possible.
  • The same habit turns harmful when work becomes an excuse to defer obligations to people who actually need attention.
  • Recommendation systems and AI slop optimize for familiarity and frictionless engagement, which produces zombie flow rather than satisfaction.
